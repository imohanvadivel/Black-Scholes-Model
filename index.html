For years people have been trading options contracts without any theoretical model for price discovery. In 1973 Fisher Black and Myron Scholes first published the Black-Scholes Model, then Robert C Merton developed the model and applied a complete mathematical understanding to the pricing formula. This pricing model is considered sacred in the financial market, so much so that all three of them received the 1997 Noble Prize for Economic Sciences.

The formula involves complex math. If you are a math nerd or curious about how it works, check out the formula or source code for this application.
